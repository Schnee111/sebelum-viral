## Main Characters

### 1. NALA

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Full Name** | Nala (default, player-changeable) |
| **Role** | Player Character — Junior Reporter, Majalah Dinding (Mading) |
| **Class** | XI IPA 2 |
| **Age** | 16 |
| **Voice** | First-person narration; curious but measured |

**Personality Traits:**
- Observant and detail-oriented
- Naturally curious but not reckless
- Empathetic — tends to see multiple sides
- Slightly insecure about being "just" a junior reporter
- Determined when something feels wrong

**Motivation:**
- Wants to prove they can handle a real story
- Genuinely believes students deserve the truth before voting
- Internal conflict: does finding the truth mean hurting people?

**Dialogue Style (Indonesian):**
- Polite but not stiff; uses "aku" and casual Bahasa gaul
- Asks a lot of questions — the player's voice of investigation
- Internal monologue is more reflective than spoken lines

**Example Lines:**
> "Aku nggak mau cuma jadi tukang gosip. Kalau ini berita, harus bisa dipertanggungjawabkan."

> "Tunggu... ada yang nggak nyambung di sini. Kenapa ceritanya berubah-ubah?"

> "Tiga kandidat, tiga rumor. Terlalu kebetulan nggak, sih?"

**Expressions Needed:**
- `nala_neutral` — default listening/exploring
- `nala_curious` — leaning in, eyes wide, during clue discovery
- `nala_worried` — hand on chin, brow furrowed, during moral dilemma
- `nala_determined` — clenched fist or firm gaze, during key decisions

### 2. LALA PRADIPTA

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Full Name** | Lala Pradipta |
| **Role** | Senior Editor, Majalah Dinding (Mading) |
| **Class** | XII IPS 1 |
| **Age** | 17 |
| **Voice** | Confident, fast-paced, occasionally bossy |

**Personality Traits:**
- Ambitious and driven — wants mading to be taken seriously
- Naturally charismatic; good at rallying people
- Has a journalist's instinct but struggles with patience
- Genuinely cares about Nala, though she pushes hard
- Prone to prioritizing speed over accuracy under pressure

**Motivation:**
- Build the mading's reputation as a legitimate student publication
- Get the "big story" before the election
- Deep down: fears being irrelevant or overlooked

**Relationship to Others:**
- **Nala:** Mentor-figure; pushes them hard but believes in them
- **Aldi/Bintang/Citra:** Sources; she sees them as stories first, people second (arc: learns to see them as people)
- **Pak Ardi:** Tense — he's skeptical of student journalism

**Dialogue Style (Indonesian):**
- Fast, energetic, uses modern slang
- Gives commands more than suggestions
- Occasionally drops wisdom that sounds surprisingly mature

**Example Lines:**
> "Nala, kita nggak punya waktu buat baper. Ini berita, bukan sinetron."

> "Dengar, kalau mau jadi jurnalis, kamu harus bisa bedain fakta sama opini. Sekarang, mana yang kita punya?"

> "Oke, aku nggak mau publish sesuatu yang nggak jelas sumbernya. Tapi... kita juga nggak boleh kalah sama akun anonim itu."

**Expressions Needed:**
- `lala_neutral` — reading/writing, focused
- `lala_excited` — grinning, leaning forward, when she smells a lead
- `lala_serious` — arms crossed, direct eye contact, editorial mode
- `lala_annoyed` — pinching nose bridge, when Nala is slow or sources are uncooperative

## OSIS Candidates

### 3. ALDI PRATAMA

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|

| **Role** | OSIS Candidate (Popular Faction) |
| **Class** | XI IPA 1 |
| **Age** | 16 |
| **Voice** | Easygoing, confident, slightly defensive when cornered |

**Personality Traits:**
- Popular and naturally charismatic — the "golden boy" type
- Genuinely wants to make school life more fun
- A bit careless with details; big-picture thinker
- Not corrupt, but sloppy with processes
- Has trouble admitting mistakes

**Motivation:**
- Win the OSIS election and prove he's a leader, not just "the popular kid"
- Wants to be taken seriously beyond his social status
- Genuinely cares about student activities

**The Rumor:**
- **@suarakelas12 claim:** Misused OSIS funds for personal benefit

- **Rendra:** Former OSIS chair; Aldi looks up to him

- Casual, uses humor to deflect

**Expressions Needed:**
- `aldi_neutral` — relaxed smile, standing casually
- `aldi_confident` — big grin, chest out, during campaign mode
- `aldi_confused` — scratching head, genuinely lost when accused
- `aldi_defensive` — tense jaw, arms crossed, when cornered about funds

## The Antagonist Trio — @suarakelas12

> **Anonymous Account:** @suarakelas12  
> **Platform:** Social media (fictional, similar to anonymous confession pages)  
> **Shared Trait:** All three use the account to advance personal agendas under the cover of "just sharing what students are saying"  
> **Key Design Note:** They are not cartoonish villains — each has a human motivation. The game's message is that misinformation comes from understandable (if wrong) human impulses.

## Character Arc Summary — Chapter 1

| Character | Chapter 1 Arc |
|---|---|
| **Nala** | Starts as eager junior reporter → encounters the three rumors → learns that the truth is layered and messy → first major choice: publish what you have or keep digging? |

## Writing Guidelines for Indonesian Dialogue

1. **Register:** Students use casual Bahasa Indonesia with modern slang (gaul). Teachers use more formal register but not stiff.
2. **Pronouns:** "gue/guwe" (casual self), "aku" (Nala's default, slightly softer), "lo/lu" (casual "you"), "kamu" (respectful), "Bapak/Ibu" (for teachers)
3. **Code-switching:** Students naturally mix Indonesian and English words ("Honestly gue bingung", "It's complicated"). Don't force it — let it flow.
4. **Generational tone:** Gen-Z Indonesian students use shorter sentences, more emoji-like expressions in speech ("Ya ampun", "Gimana sih", "Bukan main")
5. **Avoid:** Overly formal speech for students, cringe-worthy outdated slang, or stereotypes
